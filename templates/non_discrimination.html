Non-Discrimination Policy

Girls of Steel Robotics is committed to providing an inclusive and welcoming environment for all members of our community.

Policy Statement

Girls of Steel Robotics does not discriminate in admission, participation, employment, or administration of its programs or activities on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, handicap or disability, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, religion, creed, ancestry, belief, veteran status, or genetic information, or any other characteristic protected by law.

CMU Partnership

As an outreach program of Carnegie Mellon University, we adhere to the university's broader commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion. We strive to ensure that all students, mentors, and volunteers have equal access to our educational resources and opportunities.

Reporting Concerns

If you believe you have experienced discrimination or harassment within a Girls of Steel program, we encourage you to report your concerns to the program leadership or through Carnegie Mellon University's official reporting channels.
